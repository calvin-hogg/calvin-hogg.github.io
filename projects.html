Te Auaha Remake
A realistic recreation of a real environment

This is a CGI remake of Te Auaha, with all proportions matched to their real-life equivalents.

The environment was originally planned for an animated short film, featuring a more advanced version of the Dylan remake. This was later replaced by a recreation of a particular British author.
Dylan Nathaniel-Jones
A CGI recreation of a real person

This was an attempt to recreate a real person in CGI. Due to time limits, I left out his hair and ears, but this is covered by his beanie.

The character featured a simple rig, which allowed me to integrate him into the Mount Victoria environment. The clothes are simulated with Maya's nCloth.
Cyberthug and Core
A fictional species and artefact set in a dystopian future

The character and artefact for this project were created first in physical form. This gave me source material to work with when remaking them in CGI, which in turn allowed me to demonstrate effects that wouldn't have been possible otherwise (for instance, the forcefield effect).

The design of the artefact makes abundant use of hexagons, the strongest shape in nature, with the gaps in the floor giving the impression of the hive's energy spreading out of the room. The yellow and blue creates contrast between the main area and the lower deck.

The assignment required writing backstory for the species, which gave me a glimpse into the creative writing aspect that complements the visuals.
